BACKGROUND INFORMATION

Agriculture is one of East Africa's most important sectors, with about 80% of the population living in rural areas and depending on agriculture for their livelihoods(East African Community(EAC),2015). KABANA WOMEN FARMERS is a project funded by The Bill & Melinda Foundation by a grant to plant production systems, Germany Feration who lead the project with other partners in Rwanda, Kenya and Ghana

OUR VISION

Empowered Farmers For Sustainable Development.

OUR MISSION

To organise, advocate for favourable policies and transform farmers into prosperous communities.

OUR GOAL

To contribute to improved farmers' livelihoods and general agricultural development in Uganda.

OBJECTIVES OF THE ORGANISATION

  • Income generation
  • To reduce poverty
  • To create employment/self employment
  • Food security
  • Social welfare activities

OUR SERVICES

Our projects in Uganda offer a wide range of support.From helping coffee farmers in the western region to engage with markets, to enabling small-scale chilli growers in the northern region to capitalize on the sector's huge market demand, to supporting pastoralist women set up sustainable goat-rearing enterprises.
